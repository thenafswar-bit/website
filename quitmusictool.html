3-Week Quit Music Challenge

Week 1: Understand the Harm

Complete all tasks to unlock Week 2.

1
Read Through the Material
Read through the side effects and stories material on this website to understand the harmful effects of music.
2
Book Recommendation
Read "The Music Made Me Do It" - available for free on Chrome. This will give you deep insights into how music affects the mind.
3
Research Musicians' Lives
Research the lives of famous musicians. You'll find that most struggle with depression, addiction, and internal pain. This is not the life they portray on stage.
4
Write Down Your Why
Write down your personal reasons for quitting music. This can be in your Notes app, diary, or on paper. Somewhere you can revisit when urges hit. Make it specific and meaningful.
5
niyat and Quran
Listen to at least two verses of the Quran every single day. This is your foundation. It fills your heart with what truly matters , make your niyyah to quit and ask Allah swt for help.

Week 2: Identify Your Triggers

Complete all tasks to unlock Week 3.

1
Know Your Triggers
Observe what makes you want to listen to music. Pay attention to your emotions, reasons, occasions, and physical settings. When do you reach for music? Why?
2
Review Trigger Solutions
Visit the triggers page on this website and understand the roadmap for handling each trigger. Look at their solutions and see how you can apply them to your situation.
3
Increase Quran Intake
Increase the amount of Quran you listen to. It could be 5 verses, 10 verses, or more. Whatever feels right for you. The Quran is your replacement for music.

Week 3: Replace & Substitute

Complete these tasks and start your tracker journey.

1
Replace With Podcasts
Listen to Islamic podcasts instead of music. Options include: The 11th Hour Podcast, Suleman Series, Dr Haifa Series, or any science/educational podcast you like.
2
Listen to Nasheeds
Explore Islamic Nasheeds. Examples: siedd, Muslim Billal, ahmad bukhatir,Mishary rashid Al fasy,zain bhik. Find creators and nasheeds that inspire you spiritually. You can add your own examples too.
3
Urge Management
When you get a bad urge to listen to music, use the Urge Button. For really bad urges, listen to acapella versions of the songs you used to listen to. It's a step down, but it helps.
4
Tell a Friend
Tell one trusted friend about this challenge. Ask them to support you and check in with you. Start the challenge together if possible. Accountability is powerful.
5
Start Your Tracker
Use the tracker below to log each day of your journey. Every day you don't listen to music is a victory. Celebrate it.

You're Having an Urge

This feeling is temporary. It will pass. Here's what to do right now:

  • 1. Stop what you're doing - Put your phone down for 30 seconds
  • 2. Take 3 deep breaths - In through your nose, out through your mouth
  • 3. Say "Astaghfirulah wa atubu ilayh" - Seek Allah's forgiveness
  • 4. Make wudu - Purify yourself
  • 5. Pray 2 raka'ats - Connect with Allah
  • 6. Open the Quran - Let Allah's words fill your heart
  • 7. Call your friend - Tell someone who supports you
