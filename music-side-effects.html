Some of the side effects
of music

1
The Silence Anxiety
You find it impossible to do basic tasks like driving, showering, or walking to the kitchen without a background track. Silence feels loud or uncomfortable because it forces you to face your own thoughts and your distance from Allah. The moment the music stops, panic sets in.
2
The Last Third Distraction
When you try to pray at night or make Dua, lyrics from the last song you heard keep looping in your mind. Your heart is trying to connect with the Creator, but your brain is still replaying a catchy chorus, affecting your Khushu and leaving you disconnected from the moment.
3
The Cinematic Escape
You intentionally play specific songs to feel a certain way. You use music to romanticize your sadness or your anger, turning a real-life struggle into a cinematic scene rather than taking that emotion to the prayer mat for healing. The music becomes your therapist, and Allah becomes a backup plan.
4
Quranic Boredom
You feel a sense of guilt because you find the recitation of the Quran dry or plain compared to your playlists. You might find yourself scrolling through reciters looking for one that hits like a song, realizing your heart has become desensitized to the pure Word of Allah.
5
The Notification Jump
Your mood is highly volatile and dependent on your audio environment. If your headphones die or you're in a place without music, you feel irritable, drained, or low energy, as if your emotional batteries are tied to an external digital file rather than your internal state.
6
The Halal-ish Justification
You spend a significant amount of time looking for "Lo-fi Islamic" beats or "Nasheeds" that sound exactly like mainstream pop. You are trying to find loopholes to keep the high of the music while convincing your conscience that it's spiritually acceptable. The compromise is really just a delayed surrender.
7
Emotional Dependency
You've stopped knowing how to process your feelings without a soundtrack. If you are sad, you don't make Sabr; you listen to a sad song. If you are happy, you don't say Alhamdulillah; you play an upbeat track. The music has become the middleman between you and your own emotions.
8
Rushing the Salah
You view your five daily prayers as commercial breaks in your day. You rush through the Sajdah just so you can get back to your podcast, your video, or your music, treating the conversation with the Creator of this universe as a hurdle to your entertainment.
9
Lyrics as Truth
You find yourself quoting song lyrics to describe your life philosophy more often than you quote the Quran or the Hadith. The confused, often sinful perspectives of singers begin to color your worldview, making the Sunnah feel outdated or too strict by comparison.
10
The Post-Music Crash
After a long session of listening, you feel a deep, heavy emptiness when the sound stops. This is the spiritual anesthesia wearing off; the music didn't fix your loneliness or your pain. It just delayed your awareness of it, leaving the wound wider and deeper than before.
11
You Become What You Listen To
The character traits you absorb from music shape who you are without you even noticing it happening. Listen to songs that make you feel like a "boss lady" with arrogance as a feature, and you begin to see roasting people as a medal of honor. Your playlist becomes your personality. Your taste in music becomes your morality, and by the time you realize it, the change has already happened.
